Lessons

c1

Intro to CG
Computer Graphics is the art and science of creating visual content using computers. It combines mathematical algorithms, artistic principles, and programming to render images, animations, and interactive visuals that are essential in gaming, film, design, and scientific visualization.
Basic Concepts
Learn about pixels, vectors, coordinate systems, and color models that form the foundation of computer graphics.
Rendering Techniques
Explore different methods for creating images from 3D models, including rasterization and ray tracing.
Shading Models
Understanding how light interacts with surfaces and how to simulate realistic lighting in computer graphics.
Color Theory
Explore the principles of color mixing, harmony, and perception that inform effective visual design in computer graphics.
Transformations
Learn about translation, rotation, scaling, and other geometric transformations essential for manipulating objects in 3D space.
Perspective
Understanding how to simulate depth and spatial relationships through perspective projection techniques.

c2

3D Modeling
Learn techniques for creating three-dimensional digital objects and environments.
Animation Principles
Discover the fundamentals of creating movement and bringing graphics to life.
Texturing
Apply detailed surface properties to 3D models to enhance realism and visual appeal.
Rigging
Set up skeletal structures and controls for animating characters and complex objects with realistic movement.
Character Design
Principles and techniques for designing appealing and functional characters for games and animation.
Environment Design
Create immersive 3D worlds with attention to composition, lighting, and atmospheric effects.
Motion Capture
Use technology to record real-world movements and apply them to digital characters for natural animation.
Digital Sculpting
Master organic modeling techniques that mimic traditional clay sculpting in a digital environment.

c3

Real-time Graphics
Techniques for rendering graphics at interactive frame rates for games and simulations.
GPU Programming
Harness the power of graphics processing units to accelerate rendering and computations.
Advanced Effects
Create sophisticated visual effects like shadows, reflections, and particle systems.
VR/AR Graphics
Specialized techniques for creating immersive virtual and augmented reality experiences.
Procedural Generation
Algorithmically create complex models, textures, and environments without manual modeling.
Global Illumination
Advanced lighting techniques that simulate how light bounces between surfaces for realistic lighting.
Volumetric Rendering
Render atmospheric effects like fog, clouds, and fire that have volume rather than just surface properties.
